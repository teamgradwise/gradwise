How Gradwise Works

Four steps from confusion to clarity on your college financial decision.

1
Search your schools
Type any college name. We pull live data directly from the U.S. Department of Education College Scorecard — tuition, room & board, average net price, median graduate debt, graduation rates, and admissions data for 6,000+ accredited schools.
2
Select your major and career path
Choose from 10 major categories and 60+ career paths. Each path is mapped to real Bureau of Labor Statistics salary data, so your debt-to-income calculation reflects what graduates in your field actually earn.
3
Model your scenario
Adjust your expected starting salary, loan interest rate, and repayment term to stress-test different outcomes. See how your monthly payment and value score change under conservative, realistic, and optimistic assumptions.
4
See your personalized results
Schools are ranked by the Gradwise Value Score — a composite of affordability, earning power, graduation rate, selectivity, and network strength. The school that makes the best financial sense for your specific situation rises to the top.

Value Score Methodology

How we calculate the Gradwise Value Score — transparent, personalized, data-driven.

Affordability30 pts
Your debt-to-income ratio given your specific scenario. DTI under 10% = near max score. Over 20% = near zero. The most heavily weighted factor because it directly determines your post-graduation financial freedom.
Earning Power25 pts
Each school's actual graduate early-career salary outcomes from College Scorecard, compared to your expected salary from your chosen career path. Schools whose graduates consistently out-earn your benchmark score higher.
Graduation Rate20 pts
A degree you don't finish is debt without the return. 150% completion rate from College Scorecard. 95% grad rate = near max. 55% = much lower.
Selectivity15 pts
Lower acceptance rates signal stronger brand value, alumni networks, and employer recognition. Reflects real-world value of institutional prestige in hiring.
Network Strength10 pts
A composite score reflecting alumni density in professional networks, employer recruitment presence, and career services strength based on Payscale and alumni outcome data.

About Gradwise

Built to give every student the financial clarity they deserve before committing to college.

Gradwise was built out of a frustration shared by millions of students and families: the real cost of college is almost impossible to understand until it's too late. Financial aid letters are confusing. Loan terms are buried in fine print. Nobody shows you what your monthly payment will actually be on your first-year salary.

This tool exists to change that. By pulling live data directly from the U.S. Department of Education and connecting it to real salary outcomes by career path, Gradwise gives you the complete financial picture before you commit to one of the biggest decisions of your life.

Gradwise is free, requires no sign-up, and never stores or sells your data.
