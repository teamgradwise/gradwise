How Gradwise Works

Four steps from confusion to clarity on your college financial decision.

1
Search your schools
Type any college name. We pull live data from the U.S. Department of Education College Scorecard — tuition, room & board, average net price, median graduate debt, graduation rates, and admissions data for 6,000+ accredited institutions.
2
Select your major & career path
Choose from 10 major categories and 60+ career paths. Each path is mapped to real BLS salary data, so your debt-to-income calculation reflects what graduates in your field actually earn — not a generic average.
3
Model your scenario
Adjust your expected salary, interest rate, and repayment term to test conservative, realistic, and optimistic outcomes. Everything updates in real time — including the value scores.
4
See your ranked results
Schools are ranked by the Gradwise Value Score — a composite of affordability, earning power, graduation rate, selectivity, and network strength. The best financial decision for your situation rises to the top, not just the most prestigious school.

Value Score Methodology

How we calculate the Gradwise Value Score — transparent, data-driven, personalized.

Affordability30 points
Based on your debt-to-income ratio given your specific loan scenario and expected salary. A DTI under 10% scores near maximum. Above 20% scores near zero. This is the most heavily weighted factor because it directly determines your post-graduation financial freedom.
Earning Power25 points
Compares each school's actual graduate early-career salary outcomes (from College Scorecard) to your expected salary from your chosen career path. Schools whose graduates consistently out-earn your benchmark score higher.
Graduation Rate20 points
A school with a 95% graduation rate gets full marks. One with a 55% rate scores much lower. This matters because a degree you don't finish is debt without the return. Source: College Scorecard 150% completion rate.
Selectivity / Prestige15 points
Lower acceptance rates signal stronger brand value, alumni networks, and employer recognition. A 4% admit rate scores near maximum. An 80% rate scores near zero. This reflects the real-world value of institutional prestige in hiring.
Network Strength10 points
A composite score reflecting alumni density in professional networks, employer recruitment presence, and career services strength. Based on Payscale and LinkedIn alumni outcome data.

About Gradwise

Built by a student, for students — because this tool should have existed already.

Gradwise was built out of a frustration shared by millions of students and families: the real cost of college is almost impossible to understand until it's too late. Financial aid letters are confusing. Loan terms are buried in fine print. And nobody shows you what your monthly payment will actually be on a $45,000 salary.

This tool exists to change that. By pulling live data directly from the U.S. Department of Education and connecting it to real salary outcomes by career path, Gradwise gives you the complete financial picture before you commit to one of the biggest decisions of your life.

Gradwise is free, requires no sign-up, and never stores or sells your data.
